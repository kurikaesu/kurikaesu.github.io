Headphone Measurements

23 Nov 2019

It’s been a while since I’ve written anything, lots of things have happened but mostly work related things that I can’t quite talk about. Today though, I do have something I can talk about!

I managed to get a hold of some headphone measurement tools, namely the EARS by miniDSP Those who know me well know that I own a plethora of headphones and I like audio in general. For the longest time though, I longed for the ability to actually measure what my headphones were like so that I could objectively understand what was going on between the different headphone models and makes. Now that I have that measurement tool, though definitely nowhere near professional quality like the G.R.A.S stuff you should be using to take proper measurements, I put my headphones up to the task.

What did I find? A lot of interesting things actually. Its one thing to shop for headphones and look at sites like inner fidelity to find out their frequency response and gauge what the sound will be like, and another to actuall measure what you have and know that it is different from what other headphones will be, even if they are the same model. Secondly, having a measurement tool means that I can actually perform modifications like switching out earcups, pads, and even equalizing and see how it affects the headphone objectively.
